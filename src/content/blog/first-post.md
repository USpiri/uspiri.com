There's a version of me that's been meaning to write for years. This is me stopping the procrastination.

The idea is simple: if I'm already thinking through problems — in code, in conversations, in late-night rabbit holes — I might as well write some of it down. Not for an audience, at least not primarily. For clarity. Writing forces a kind of precision that thinking alone rarely demands.

I've read enough "why I started a blog" posts to know this opening is a cliché. So let's skip ahead.

## What this is

A place for notes that are too long for a thread and too informal for a proper article. Development, design, the occasional opinion on tools I use or build. Maybe some things adjacent to code — architecture decisions, things I got wrong, things I got surprisingly right.

No fixed schedule. No SEO strategy. No newsletter (yet).

## What this isn't

A portfolio. I already have one of those. This is the scratchpad that lives next to it — messier, more honest, more exploratory.

If something here is useful to you, great. If not, that's fine too. I'm mostly writing for the future version of myself who forgets things.
